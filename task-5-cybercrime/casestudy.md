UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India. In this type of fraud, scammers trick users into revealing sensitive information or approving fake transactions.

The fraud usually begins when the victim receives a call, message, or link pretending to be from a bank, delivery service, or customer support. The scammer may ask the user to click on a link or send a payment request. In many cases, the victim is asked to enter their UPI PIN or approve a “receive money” request, which actually results in money being deducted from their account.

Students and elderly people are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences include financial loss, mental stress, and misuse of personal data.

For example, a student trying to sell an item online may receive a fake payment request from a buyer. Thinking it is a genuine payment, the student approves the request and ends up losing money. This shows how easily people can be misled without proper awareness.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious while using online payment systems and never sharing sensitive details. I found this topic important because many students are unaware of such scams.
